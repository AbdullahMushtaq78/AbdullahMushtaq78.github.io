About

I am an AI researcher currently working as a Visiting Research Assistant at Qatar University, Doha, Qatar, under the supervision of Ezieddin Elmahjub (Harvard & Qatar University), Junaid Qadir, and Ibrahim Ghaznavi.

My research spans LLM inference and benchmarking, dynamic multi-agent systems, agentic planning, reducing LLM hallucination and sycophancy, alignment, and multimodal deep learning. I also work on reinforcement learning, world models, and simulations.

I have research experience and collaborations with internationally recognized institutions including Oxford, Weill Cornell Medicine, Imperial College London, Zayed University, Qatar University, Copenhagen Business School, LUMS, and ITU Lahore. I have authored multiple peer-reviewed publications at top-tier venues including JAIR (Q1, H-index: 143) and NeurIPS 2025, with additional work under review at Artificial Intelligence and Law.

I hold a B.S. in Computer Science from Information Technology University, Lahore, graduating in the top 5% on the Dean's Honor List with a specialization GPA of 3.83/4.0. I am a recipient of the nationally competitive HEC Ehsaas fully-funded undergraduate scholarship and the NGIRI 2023–24 national ICT research grant (IGNITE). I have served as a Reviewer at NeurIPS 2025 and as a Teaching Assistant for three courses at ITU, supporting 250+ students.
