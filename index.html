About Me

I am an AI researcher currently working as a Visiting Research Assistant at Qatar University, Doha, Qatar, supervised by Ezieddin Elmahjub (Harvard & Qatar University) and Junaid Qadir. My core research interests span LLM inference and benchmarking, dynamic multi-agent systems, agentic planning, alignment, and reducing hallucination and sycophancy in large language models. I also work on reinforcement learning for LLMs, world models, and multimodal deep learning.

I have research collaborations with internationally recognized institutions including Oxford, Weill Cornell Medicine, Imperial College London, Zayed University, Qatar University, Copenhagen Business School, LUMS, and ITU Lahore. I have authored multiple peer-reviewed publications at top-tier venues including JAIR (Q1, H-index: 143) and NeurIPS 2025, with additional work under review at Artificial Intelligence and Law.

I hold a B.S. in Computer Science from Information Technology University, Lahore (2020–2024), graduating in the top 5% on the Dean's Honor List with a specialization GPA of 3.83/4.0. I am a recipient of the nationally competitive HEC Ehsaas fully-funded undergraduate scholarship and the NGIRI 2023–24 national ICT research grant (IGNITE).
